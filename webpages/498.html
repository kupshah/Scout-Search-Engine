The Independent Garden Center Show (IGC Show) is held every year in Chicago, IL, in the United States. It was created by husband-and-wife founders and producers Jeff and Cheryl Morey, who are also co-directors of Garden Centers of America (GCA)[1] and Publishers of IGC Magazine.[2] The IGC Show is the world’s largest trade show, conference and networking event for independent garden center owners, managers and buyers. It is geared toward independents only - no big-box stores are represented or allowed into the event. It is not open to the public.[2]

The IGC Show presents garden center products available at wholesale from around the world. It is a juried show - this means that every exhibiting company has been screened to ensure the products offered for sale are relevant to independent garden centers.[2]

IGC retailers have access to over 30 hours of educational sessions at the IGC Retailer Conference, covering a range of topics important to IGCs.

In addition to the trade show, the IGC Show features an educational conference on topics relevant to garden centers, such as customer service, inventory, signage, social media, store design, advertising and more. Each day before the trade show opens, a keynote speaker, who is a prominent figure in the garden center industry or an expert in business or a related field, starts the day with inspirational insights.[2]

The IGC Show offers a newly redesigned networking hub, the IGC Networking Lounge, located in the center of the main trade show hall for garden retailers to connect with their fellow peers and charge their portable devices. "Shop Talk" Retail Discussions, located in the IGC Networking Lounge and facilitated by a moderator, give retailers the opportunity to share their thoughts on a range of garden retailing topics, including marketing strategies, minimum wage increases and getting younger customers shopping.[3]

Aerial view of the IGC Networking Lounge, where garden center owners and buyers gather to discuss retail-relevant topics.
Garden center retailers express their opinions with their peers during the moderated "Shop Talk" Retail Discussions in the IGC Networking Lounge on topics most relevant to garden retailing.

The New Vendor Zone, located en route to the Aon Grand Ballroom, presents garden center buyers, managers and owners with a sampling of companies that are new to the IGC Show and garden center industry. The New Product Zone features new products and plants submitted by vendors all across the show's exhibit space. A special contest was held to award the Best New Product and the Best New Plant featured in this zone. Garden center retailers voted for their favorite products and plants, and the first-place and runner-up products and plants received award ribbons.[4] The first place Best New Product award went to Kreative Gardens of Miami, FL; the second place Best New Product award went to Potwheelz Garden Dolly of Gulf Breeze, FL.[5] The first place Best New Plant award went to Regal Petticoat Maple from Sester Farms in Gresham, OR; the second place Best New Plant award went to 'Gold Breeze' Zebra Grass from Hortech in Spring Lake, MI.[5]

History[edit]

Aerial view of the IGC Chicago trade show floor, August 18–20, 2015.

The first IGC Show was held on August 21–23, 2007, at Chicago’s Navy Pier, and IGC Show continues to convene at this waterfront venue each year. [2]

In 2014, the show organizers started hosting the IGC Show at a second location for the industry’s Eastern market. In 2014, IGC East convened August 5–7 at the Gaylord National Resort and Convention Center in National Harbor, MD.[6] In 2015, IGC East relocated to the Baltimore Convention Center in Baltimore, MD.[7] In 2016, the East Coast event relocated to the Valley Forge Casino Resort August 2–4.[8] It featured a new format targeting the special interests of Eastern IGC retailers with regionally focused keynotes, educational conference sessions and networking opportunities, including Tuesday evening’s concert.[9]

IGC Show reconvenes at Chicago's Navy Pier, August 14-16, 2018.[5]

IGC Show is the world's biggest buying and education event for independent garden centers.

Founders[edit]

Jeff Morey, along with his wife, Cheryl, is the Founder and Producer of the IGC Show, entering its 11th year. Jeff is the Co-director, with Cheryl, of Garden Centers of America, the nation’s only association focused exclusively on the needs of independent garden centers.[2] As the hands-on second-generation CEO and Publisher of IGC Magazine, the garden center industry's leading B-2-B publication, Jeff carries on the tradition of editorial excellence founded by his late father, Richard Morey. He draws upon 40-plus years of experience, which includes business development, marketing and editorial direction, to inspire the IGC industry to think outside the box.[2] Jeff is known for his targeted on-stage interviews with today’s thought-leaders, including Martha Stewart, Greenpeace Co-founder Dr. Patrick Moore, best-selling author and retail visionary Paco Underhill, and lifestyle celebrities such as P. Allen Smith, Jamie Durie and U.K. garden design genius Diarmuid Gavin. He has appeared on BBC television in the U.K.

IGC Magazine[edit]

IGC Magazine, the garden center industry's business-to-business publication.[22] The magazine is published bi-monthly.[22]

Garden Centers of America (GCA)[edit]

Garden Centers of America (GCA) is the nation’s only association focused exclusively on the needs of independent garden centers.[23] The association provides means of networking, touring other garden center facilities, monthly e-newsletters, member profiles, and coverage of the horticulture and floriculture industry nationwide.[23]
